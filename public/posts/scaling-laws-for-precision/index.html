Scaling Laws for Precision

Abstract

The main contribution of this paper is devise “precision-aware” scaling laws for training and inference. It comes from observations that the degradation introduced by post-training quantization increases as models are trained on more data, which demands for scaling law to tell us the data should be used. For training, scaling laws suggest that training larger models in lower precision may be compute optimal. They unify the scaling laws for post and pre-training quantization to arrive at a single functional form that predicts degradation from training and inference in varied precisions.
